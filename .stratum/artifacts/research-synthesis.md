## Research Synthesis — Shirl's Event Planning & Rentals

Version: v1
Built against: none
Changed since v1: initial version

### Key Questions
- Do individuals and small businesses in King/Snohomish counties actually feel the planning + rental-coordination pain, or is this an assumed problem?
- Is "businesses and individuals" one segment, or two distinct buyer types with different needs?
- Would a real online presence meaningfully expand Shirl's reach beyond word-of-mouth?
- Would customers prefer one bundled local vendor (planning + rentals) over assembling multiple vendors themselves?
- Are individuals/businesses willing to pay a premium for that bundle?
- Who already serves this market, and where are the open gaps?

### Hypotheses (HITS)

**H1 — Problem (Individuals):** We believe individuals planning one-time life events (weddings, birthday milestones, graduations, anniversaries) experience real friction from having to separately plan the event *and* source equipment (chairs, tables, centerpieces, linens), and would use a combined planning + rental service if it removed that coordination burden.

**H2 — Problem (Businesses):** We believe small businesses/organizations that host occasional events (holiday parties, fundraisers, client appreciation events) don't want to own event equipment because they use it infrequently, and would rather rent locally than buy or store it themselves.

**H3 — User (Reach beyond word of mouth):** We believe Shirl's current word-of-mouth/social-media customer base is a narrow slice of the local market, and that people with the same need who don't already know her personally are currently going elsewhere — or not being served at all — because she has no discoverable online presence.

**H4 — Solution (Bundled offering):** We believe customers currently cobble together separate sources for planning and rentals (buying disposables, borrowing, big-box rental chains, separate planners) and would prefer one local vendor that bundles coordination and inventory together.

**H5 — Business (Willingness to pay for the bundle):** We believe individuals and small businesses are willing to pay more for a single local vendor that bundles planning + rental versus assembling vendors themselves.

### Interview Questions

**H1 — Individuals**
1. Tell me about the last event you planned for yourself or your family — what kind was it?
2. Walk me through everything you had to do to make it happen, start to finish.
3. Where did you get your tables, chairs, linens, centerpieces, etc.? How did you decide?
4. Tell me about a specific moment in the planning that was stressful or frustrating.
5. How many different vendors or people did you have to coordinate with?
6. Tell me about a time equipment didn't show up, or you had to scramble at the last minute.
7. Roughly how much time did you spend on logistics versus actually enjoying the event?

**H2 — Businesses**
1. Tell me about the last event your business/organization hosted that needed rented equipment.
2. How did you source that equipment — buy, rent, borrow? Walk me through that decision.
3. Where do you store event equipment, if you own any? Tell me about a time storage was a problem.
4. Tell me about the last time you planned an event on a tight budget or timeline.
5. Who's responsible for event logistics at your org, and how much time does it eat up?
6. Walk me through your experience with a rental company you've used before, good or bad.
7. Tell me about a time an event went sideways because of an equipment or logistics issue.

**H3 — Reach**
1. Think of the last time you needed a vendor you'd never used before — how did you search?
2. Tell me about the last time you searched online for an event planner or rental company. What did you search, what did you click?
3. How did you first hear about the vendors you've used for past events?
4. Tell me about a time you almost hired someone but backed out because you couldn't find enough info about them online.
5. Do you know Shirl or her business already? If yes, how'd you hear about her? If no — would you have found her before this conversation?

**H4 — Bundling**
1. Walk me through every vendor/source you used for the last event you were involved in.
2. Tell me about a time you had to coordinate between a planner and a separate rental company.
3. Have you ever bought event items instead of renting? What drove that decision?
4. Tell me about the last time you borrowed equipment from friends or family — how'd that go?
5. If you've used a national rental chain, what was that like compared to working with someone local?

**H5 — Willingness to pay**
1. Tell me about the last time you paid extra for convenience — what was it, why worth it?
2. Walk me through the budget for your last event — what was worth spending more on vs. cutting?
3. Have you ever paid a bundled "package" price for services (catering + venue, etc.)? How'd that feel?
4. Tell me about the last time you shopped around or negotiated on price for event services.

**Note on existing data:** Shirl already has informal evidence sitting in her social media DMs and past customer inquiries — worth reviewing before formal interviews to see what people already ask about, push back on, or book for. A pricing-sensitivity survey or landing-page A/B test could validate H5 at scale later, but building that instrumentation is `/observability`'s job, not this skill's.

### Competitive Findings

| [Grand Event Rentals](https://www.grandeventrentalswa.com/) | Rentals only, catalog + quote request | Seattle / PNW, based in Bothell | Large inventory, sustainability/local-sourcing messaging, NACE/WIPA membership | No planning service; no visible pricing; based further north |
| [National Event Pros](https://nationaleventpros.com/seattle-event-planning/) | Full event planning + rentals, bundled | Seattle, Bellevue, Mercer Island, Redmond, Vancouver | Single-coordinator model, 35 yrs experience, matches Shirl's "bundle" concept | Built for bigger-budget/corporate-scale production (VR simulators, large staffing); not focused on small local individual events; doesn't reach Burien/south King Co. specifically |

**Gap identified:** No competitor found serving the Burien / south King County / south Snohomish corridor bundles planning + rentals at a small/medium, individual-event scale. The rental-only players (Alexander, Delivering Fun, GSR, Grand Event) don't plan events. The two that do bundle planning (Jashan, National Event Pros) are built for larger, higher-budget, more corporate events elsewhere in the region. Also notable: every competitor uses an opaque quote-request pricing model — none show real package pricing online.

### Hypothesis Verdicts
- H1–H5: INCONCLUSIVE — no interviews conducted yet (deferred by user request)
- Market-gap observation ("bundled local planning+rental is unoccupied at Shirl's scale in this corridor"): VALIDATED as a market-structure finding — confirmed by direct comparison of 6 local competitors, though "no one competitor exists" can never be proven exhaustively, only that none surfaced in verified search.

### Confidence Levels
- H1–H5: Low (no evidence collected yet)
- Market-gap finding: Medium (six competitors is a reasonable sample, verified live, but not an exhaustive market scan)

### PRD Implications
- Split "target user" into two personas in the Discovery Brief: Individual/Family (life events) and Small Business/Org (occasional events) — don't carry "anyone" forward as a single persona.
- Core v1 candidates: online catalog (tables, chairs, centerpieces, linens, etc.) + rental request/quote flow + planning/coordination service shown and bookable on the same site.
- Positioning: local, bundled planning + rentals, sized for small/medium one-time events — distinct from rental-only competitors (no planning) and from the two full-service competitors (built for bigger/corporate scale).
- Possible differentiator to consider deliberately: visible package pricing, since every competitor found hides pricing behind a quote request.
- Non-goal candidate for v1: large-scale corporate production, festival tenting, security/staffing — that's the Jashan/National Event Pros tier, unless Shirl already does this.
- Open Questions (carried forward since H1–H5 are Inconclusive): Is the planning+rental pain real enough to drive purchase? Which segment launches first? Will either segment actually pay a premium for the bundle? Recommend running the HITS interview guide — even a lightweight pass with 5–8 existing word-of-mouth customers — before locking core v1 scope in `/discover`.

### Key Insight
The gap isn't "no one rents event equipment near Burien" — plenty do. It's that nobody serving small/medium individual and small-business events in this specific corridor pairs rental inventory with real planning help at an accessible scale; the two bundled competitors that exist are built for bigger, corporate-budget events. That's a real positioning wedge — but it rests on demand hypotheses (H1, H2, H4, H5) that are still unvalidated. Worth testing before the business model leans on it entirely.
