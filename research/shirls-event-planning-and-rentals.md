# Shirl's Event Planning & Rentals — Research Note

**Researcher:** George Enninful
**Stakeholder:** Shirl — owner of the event planning & rentals business, decision-maker on go/no-go, segment targeting, and positioning
**Area:** New product — event planning & rentals website
**Method:** Mixed (Hypothesis-driven interview design prepared, not yet fielded) + Competitive analysis
**Participants / sample:** User & Generative — 0 interviews conducted (fieldwork deferred by stakeholder request); Competitive — 6 competitors identified and verified via live web research
**Dates:** 2026-07-13 → 2026-07-13

## TL;DR

- No competitor was found serving the Burien / south King County / south Snohomish corridor that bundles event *planning* with equipment *rentals* at a small/medium, individual-event scale — this is a real, verified market-structure gap.
- The two competitors that do bundle planning + rentals (Jashan Event Services, National Event Pros) are built for larger, higher-budget, more corporate-scale events, and neither specifically targets Shirl's service area.
- Four local rental-only competitors (Alexander Party Rentals, Delivering Fun Party Rentals, GSR Rentals, Grand Event Rentals) cover Shirl's territory but don't offer planning, and none publish real pricing online.
- Whether customers actually want the planning+rental bundle enough to choose it over assembling vendors themselves — or pay a premium for it — is still unvalidated. Five hypotheses (H1–H5) and a ready interview guide exist for this but haven't been fielded yet.
- Recommendation: treat the competitive gap as a genuine positioning opportunity, but don't lock core v1 scope until at least a lightweight round of customer conversations tests demand.

## What we wanted to learn

Whether individuals and small businesses in Shirl's service area (Burien, King & Snohomish counties) experience real friction planning one-time events and sourcing rental equipment separately, whether they'd prefer one local bundled vendor over assembling multiple vendors themselves, whether they'd pay a premium for that bundle, and whether Shirl's current word-of-mouth reach is leaving a meaningful segment of the market unserved. Five falsifiable hypotheses were drafted (HITS format) to structure this — see "What we did" for the discussion guide, and "Open questions" for their status.

## What we did

**User & Generative (not yet fielded):** Five HITS hypotheses were drafted across problem, user, solution, and business-assumption categories, each with a 5–7 question open-ended interview guide (past-behavior framing, no leading questions about future intent). Fieldwork — ~5–8 conversations with existing or prospective customers — was scoped but deferred at the stakeholder's request to keep the project moving; the guide is ready to run whenever Shirl wants to pick it up.

**Competitive analysis:** Conducted 2026-07-13 via live WebSearch/WebFetch queries targeting event/party rental and event-planning companies serving Burien, King County, and Snohomish County, WA. Six competitors were identified, and each company's own website was fetched directly to verify service offerings, coverage area, and positioning — no competitor or claim was taken from trained/recalled knowledge.

## What we found

### Finding 1: No local competitor bundles planning + rentals at Shirl's scale

Six competitors were verified: Alexander Party Rentals, Delivering Fun Party Rentals, Jashan Event Services, GSR Rentals, Grand Event Rentals, and National Event Pros. Four are rental-only with no planning/coordination service (Alexander, Delivering Fun, GSR, Grand Event). The two that do bundle planning + rentals — [Jashan Event Services](https://jashanservices.com/) and [National Event Pros](https://nationaleventpros.com/seattle-event-planning/) — are built for larger, corporate/production-scale events (e.g., National Event Pros offers VR simulators and large-scale staffing; Jashan serves the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue corridor broadly, not the Burien/south-county area specifically). Neither is positioned for small/medium individual life events in Shirl's exact service area.

### Finding 2: Rental-only competitors already cover Shirl's territory closely

[Delivering Fun Party Rentals](https://deliveringfunpartyrentals.com/rentals/linen-rentals/) explicitly lists Burien as a service area, and [GSR Rentals](https://www.gsrrentals.com/) covers the East King/Southern Snohomish side. Both are rental-only, meaning they're direct product-inventory competitors even though they don't compete on the planning side.

### Finding 3: Industry-wide, pricing is hidden behind quote requests

All six competitors verified use a "request a quote" model rather than published package pricing. This is a potential differentiator for Shirl's site (transparent pricing) or simply the accepted norm to match — a deliberate choice rather than a default.

### Finding 4: Demand for the bundle itself is unvalidated

All five HITS hypotheses (problem, user, solution, and business assumptions — see Related) remain Inconclusive; no interviews have been conducted. The competitive gap tells us the *market structure* has room for a bundled local offering, but not whether individuals and small businesses in this corridor would actually choose it, or pay more for it, over their current workarounds (buying, borrowing, big-box rental chains, separate planners).

## Implications

- **Discovery Brief — User Roles/Personas:** Split "anyone" into two explicit personas — Individual/Family (life events) and Small Business/Org (occasional events) — rather than one undifferentiated user type.
- **Discovery Brief — Core features for v1:** Consider an online catalog (tables, chairs, centerpieces, linens) plus a rental request/quote flow, with planning/coordination shown and bookable on the same site.
- **Discovery Brief — Positioning/Non-Goals:** Position as local, bundled planning + rentals for small/medium one-time events; explicitly scope out large corporate production, festival-scale tenting, and staffing/security (the Jashan/National Event Pros tier) unless Shirl already offers this.
- **Discovery Brief — Open Questions:** Carry forward H1–H5 as open questions pending fieldwork.

## Open questions

- Is the planning+rental coordination pain (H1, H2) real and strong enough to drive a purchase decision, or are people already satisfied with their current workarounds?
- Which segment — individuals or small businesses — should launch first?
- Would either segment actually pay a premium for the bundle (H5), or is price parity with rental-only competitors required?
- Would a discoverable online presence meaningfully expand reach beyond word-of-mouth (H3), or is word-of-mouth already capturing most of the addressable local market?
- Next step to find out: run the prepared HITS interview guide (5–8 conversations) with existing word-of-mouth customers and a few net-new prospects, ideally before or alongside `/discover` locks core v1 scope.
